Arduino Projects

Discovering Arduino has been an interesting journey for me. The moment I delved into the inner workings of C++ programming language for Arduino, new ideas of possibilities opened up. From that point forward. Starting with the introductory book, I learned The basics of how to work with an arduino. Some of the projects were for example: make a light switch, a door alarm, and a temperature meter. As I was working on these projects I started to understand the code more and also started to put my own ideas into the projects. 2 of the images shown down below represents the project of a light switch when it becomes dark.

Once I had a solid grasp of the basics, I eagerly began exploring various projects outlined in the book. These guided exercises not only honed my programming skills but also expanded my knowledge of Arduino. With each project completed, I felt a sense of accomplishment and a growing desire to push my creative boundaries. Therefore I and a classmate made 2 projects. (Also shown in the images)

Repeat the Beat was my favorite arduino project because we could make whatever we wanted to make. I wanted something with a rgb strip and my classmate wanted to make a minigame. We combined our ideas and brainstormed. The idea of the game is that one player sets 3 or 4 light dots with the button they can use. The rgb strip has one light that goes back and forth. When the light dot goes back the other player needs to repeat the same pattern. If failed it slows down, if succeeded it goes faster. We also made a vault once to test our c++ arduino knowledge.
